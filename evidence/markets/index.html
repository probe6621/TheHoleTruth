Frequently Asked Questions

What is mean reversion trading?

Mean reversion is a strategy based on the tendency of price to return to its average after becoming oversold or overbought. When price extends too far from fair value, buying pressure builds at lows and selling pressure builds at highs, pulling price back toward equilibrium. This indicator shows you exactly where that equilibrium is across multiple timeframes.

What is multi-timeframe (MTF) alignment?

MTF alignment happens when multiple timeframes all point the same direction ‚Äî for example, when the 1m, 5m, 15m, and 1h all show targets above current price. These "pure alignment" setups are higher probability because you're not fighting conflicting signals. The indicator highlights these moments with background color and alerts.
